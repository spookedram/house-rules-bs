Before you can start playing House Rules RPG, you'll need:

  • an Architect
  • a Quest
  • 1 or more Players
  • with at least 1 character each
  • An understanding of the rules
  • a dice set or an RNG

Taking Actions

Free Actions

During the game, Players will want their character to interact with the world and will do so by taking an action. Actions with little difficulty, such as speaking bluntly or opening an unlocked door, are free actions. If the action that the Player wants their character to take is more difficult then they'll need to roll a difficulty check.

Difficulty Checks

Throwing a knife to cut a swinging rope, disarming a ticking time bomb, and summoning an unknowable beast from another dimension are all a lot more difficult than opening an unlocked door. Actions with any level of difficulty require a difficulty check. The Player will describe the action they want to attempt and the Architect decides what modifier is being checked and how difficult the action being taken is (as a number which they may or may not share with the player). Then the Player rolls a d20 to see if they succeed or fail the action. For example:

Player: "I want to use my new lockpick on the cage."
Architect: "Alright, roll a dexterity check."
Architect (thinking): Probably a DC of 14...
Player: "Nice! I rolled a 16!"
Architect: "You pick the lock. Sadly, you've also woken the circus bear inside..."

You might have noticed that the Player's action had an ability that was being checked. When an action is likely going to result from a test of a character's abilities, they make an ability check.

Ability Scores

Every character and creature in House Rules RPG has 6 ability scores, represented by a number between 1 and 20. The abilities cover a wide variety of character attributes and actions that can be taken. These 6 abilities are:

Strength Represents muscle and physical power
Dexterity Represents agility, reflexes, and balance
Constitution Represents health, stamina, and endurance
Intelligence Represents critical thinking, learning, and logic
Wisdom Represents willpower, common sense, and intuition
Charisma Represents personality, likability, and ability to lead
