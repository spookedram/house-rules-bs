Cheat Sheet

Actions

Difficulty Checks

For difficult actions Players must roll a d20, add their character's ability score modifer to the roll, and compare the result to the action's difficuilty challenge (DC). If the result is higher they succeed, if not they fail.

Critical Rolls:
Rolling a natural 1 is a critical miss, rolling a natural 20 is a critical hit.

Advantage:
Roll twice and keep the higher roll.

Disadvantage:
Roll twice and keep the lower roll.

Ability Scores

Strength Represents muscle and physical power STR
Dexterity Represents agility, reflexes, and balance DEX
Constitution Represents health, stamina, and endurance CON
Intelligence Represents critical thinking, learning, and logic INT
Wisdom Represents willpower, common sense, and intuition WIS
Charisma Represents personality, likability, and ability to lead CHA
Perception Represents senses and awareness PER

Combat

Surprise Round:
If a character or creature is undetected, they can attack a target before initiative is set. Undetected characters/creatures become detected on a successful PER check.

Initiative:
Players roll a DEX check for each character, Game Masters roll for each type of creature. Highest goes first, lowest goes last.

Turn:
A turn is one character/creature making 1 movement and 1 action, in that order. Attacking counts as an action.

Round:
A round is completed when the initiative order comes back to the highest roll. A round represents 6 seconds in-game.

Attacking:
Attacks are essentially difficulty checks; roll a d20 and add the appropriate ability score modifier. If the result is higher that the target's AC the attack hits. If not, it misses.

Death:
If an NPC's HP hits or passes 0, they die. If a Player character's HP hits or passes 0, they fall unconcious and must roll 3 death saving throws to wake up. If they fail all 3 rolls, they die. If they succeed on a roll, they wake with 1d6 HP. Healing a character may not wake them up, but their death saving throws are reset.

Saving Throws:
Some attacks can be avoided by rolling a saving throw. The ability being checked against is determined by the nature of the attack, as seen on the Attack Types table below.
